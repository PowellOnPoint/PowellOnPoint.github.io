2 Project Overview

This project, completed as the final assignment for MSDS 6371 (Statistical Foundations for Data Science), analyzes housing prices in Ames, Iowa using multiple linear regression. The analysis addresses two distinct objectives:

  1. Client-Focused Model for Century 21 Ames: Develop a clear, interpretable model of SalePrice versus GrLivArea (scaled per 100 sq ft) for the NAmes, Edwards, and BrkSide neighborhoods, including interaction effects to account for neighborhood-specific pricing dynamics.

  2. Predictive Modeling Competition: Build and compare the strongest possible MLR models across all Ames neighborhoods for Kaggle submission, evaluating performance on unseen test data.

The project involved comprehensive data cleaning, exploratory data analysis, model building with diagnostics, influential point analysis, and cross-validation. All techniques were limited to those covered in the course.

4 Key Insights

4.1 1. Neighborhood Interactions Drive Pricing Differences

The relationship between living area (GrLivArea) and sale price is not uniform across neighborhoods. For Century 21 Ames’ target neighborhoods (NAmes, Edwards, BrkSide), interaction terms between GrLivArea and neighborhood were essential. The model revealed that a 100 sq ft increase in living area yields different price premiums depending on the neighborhood, with Edwards showing the steepest slope.

4.2 2. Quality and Size Dominate Predictive Power

While living area is important, overall quality (OverallQual) and total square footage (TotalSF) emerged as the strongest predictors across all models. Neighborhood effects were significant, but grouping neighborhoods by median sale price improved model parsimony without sacrificing performance. The best predictive model explained over 91% of variance in sale prices.

5 Predictive Modeling

Five competing multiple linear regression models were developed and rigorously evaluated using cross-validation and external validation via Kaggle submission:

  • SLR: SalePrice ~ GrLivArea (simple baseline)
  • MLR1: SalePrice ~ GrLivArea + FullBath (adding bathrooms)
  • MLR2: Full model with 25 predictors selected through EDA
  • MLR3: SalePrice ~ TotalSF + Neighborhood + OverallQual (parsimonious model)
  • MLR3.N: SalePrice ~ TotalSF + NeighborhoodGroup + OverallQual (with neighborhood grouping)

All models underwent thorough diagnostics including residual analysis, normality checks, homoscedasticity, linearity, influential point analysis (Cook’s Distance, leverage), and cross-validation (PRESS statistic).

5.1 Model Performance Comparison

Model Adjusted R² PRESS CV RMSE (log) AIC Kaggle Score (RMSE)
SLR 0.5256 111 0.1255 380.4 0.29893
MLR1 0.5574 103 0.1265 280.9 0.29090
MLR2 0.9167 Inf 0.1265 -2053.9 0.16264
MLR3 0.8638 33 0.1260 -1407.0 0.16416
MLR3.N 0.8561 34 0.1265 -1347.0 0.16663

The MLR2 model achieved the best Kaggle performance with an RMSE of 0.16264 on the log-transformed sale prices, placing it competitively on the leaderboard.
